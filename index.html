NAME DROP KNOCKOUT

A Fast-Paced Game of Famous Fights & Alphabet Battles

What’s in the Box:

  • 52 Letter Tiles (2 of each A–Z)
  • 1 Sand Timer (2-minute timer)
  • 1 Cloth Drawstring Tile Bag
  • 4 Pencils
  • Scoring Pad
  • QR Code to Digital Rulebook

Objective:

Be the first to reach 15 points by thinking fast, naming famous people with randomly drawn initials, and debating whose pick would win in a prime-time fictional fistfight.

Setup:

  1. Place all 52 tiles (2 of each letter A–Z) in the cloth bag.
  2. Give each player a pencil and a sheet of paper.
  3. Place the sand timer in reach.

Gameplay Overview:

Each round consists of drawing initials, quickly naming a person, resolving any challenges, writing names under a time limit, revealing and crossing out duplicates, choosing a champion, and debating a winner.

Round Flow (2+ Players):

  1. DRAW INITIALS:
    • Randomly draw 2 tiles from the bag.
    • The first tile drawn is the first name initial.
    • The second tile drawn is the last name initial.
    • If the combo of initials is agreed to be too hard, redraw letters.
  2. FIRST NAME CALL:
    • The first player to shout a valid person with those initials (real or fictional) gets +1 point.
    • Example: If initials are T + O, someone might call out "Terrell Owens."
    • Any repeating names between players cancel out and do not count during scoring. Players cannot write down the first name yelled unless they were the one who yelled it.
    • If no name is yelled within 2 minutes, redraw initials, no points awarded.
  3. CHALLENGE PHASE (Optional):
    • If any player thinks the first name yelled is invalid, they may call “CHALLENGE!”
    • Players then debate the validity of the name.
    • If players disagree, have the challenger Google the name and ask: “What is their job/occupation, or what is one fact about them?”
    • If the challenger is successful (the name is invalid):
      • The challenger earns +1 point.
      • The first name call does not count and the timer does not start.
      • The round ends immediately after the challenge resolution and new letters are drawn for the next round.
    • If the challenger is unsuccessful:
      • The player who yelled the name earns +1 additional point (in addition to the initial point).
      • The round ends immediately after the challenge resolution and new letters are drawn for the next round.
  4. TIMER START (2 MINUTES):
    • All players write down as many valid people as possible with the given initials.
    • You may not write down the first name called unless you were the one who yelled it.
    • No repeats from previous rounds.
  5. REVEAL & CROSS OUT DUPLICATES:
    • Players reveal their lists.
    • Any names that appear on more than one player’s list are crossed out and do not count.
  6. CHAMPION SELECTION:
    • Each player picks one valid name from their remaining list to send into the fight.
  7. DEBATE PHASE:
    • Each player argues for 2 minutes why their fighter would win.
    • Then, each has 2 minutes to rebut their opponent’s argument.
    • Players assume all fighters are in their prime.
  8. DECIDE WINNER:
    • In 2-player games:
      • If both players agree, the winner gets +1 point.
      • If no agreement, the player who did not get the First Name Call may:
        • Call a third-party judge or AI, and the winner will get 2 points.
        • Or concede the debate point to the first-namer (worth only +1 point instead of 2).
    • In 3+ player games, group votes to decide who wins the fight (or if you can't decide you can always ask AI still).
  9. BONUS POINT:
    • If only one player submitted any valid names, that player gets +1 bonus point.
  10. SCORE IT:
    • Record all points from the round.

Scoring Summary:

  • +1: First to name a valid person.
  • +1: Winner of the fight debate (unless in 2-player scenario decided by AI, then +2 total points).
  • +1: Bonus if the opponent didn’t write any names.
  • +1: Successful challenge against invalid first name.

Winning the Game:

First player to reach 15 points wins!

Round Examples:

2-Player Example:

  • Tiles Drawn: B + J
  • Player 1 shouts: "Billy Joel" (+1 point)
  • Timer starts.
  • Player 1 writes: Billy Joel, Bob Johnson, Boris Johnson
  • Player 2 writes: Betty Jones, Bruce Jenner
  • Players reveal lists and cross out duplicates.
  • Player 1 picks: Boris Johnson
  • Player 2 picks: Bruce Jenner
  • Debate happens.
  • Group votes Bruce Jenner wins (+1 to Player 2)
  • No bonus, as both submitted names.
  • Player 1: 1 point
  • Player 2: 1 point

Round Flow Chart:

[DRAW INITIALS] → [FIRST VALID NAME CALLED? (CHALLENGE POSSIBLE)] → [CHALLENGE RESOLUTION (ROUND ENDS IF CHALLENGE)] → [START TIMER] → [WRITE NAMES] → [REVEAL & CROSS OUT DUPLICATES] → [SELECT FIGHTER] → [DEBATE] → [DECIDE WINNER] → [ASSIGN POINTS] → [SCOREPAD]

Additional Rules:

  • Anyone famous or Google-able is fair game.
  • No made-up people or overly obscure names.
  • You cannot use the same name more than once during the game.
  • All fighters assumed to be in their prime.
  • Players may agree to use a random genre filter (athletes only, villains only, etc.) for extra challenge.
  • This is a fist fight — Clark Kent can't use his laser eyes, but if Bruce Banner wants to turn into The Hulk and throw someone across the globe, who's gonna stop him?
  • Speaking of The Hulk, he isn't valid for T and H — and The Rock isn't valid for T and R.

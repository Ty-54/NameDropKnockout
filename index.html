NAME DROP KNOCKOUT

A Fast-Paced Game of Famous Fights & Alphabet Battles

WHAT'S IN THE BOX:

  • 52 Letter Tiles (2 of each A–Z)
  • 1 Sand Timer (2-minute timer)
  • 1 Cloth Drawstring Tile Bag
  • 4 Pencils
  • Scoring Pad
  • QR Code to Digital Rulebook

OBJECTIVE:

Be the first to reach 15 points by thinking fast, naming famous people with randomly drawn initials, and debating whose pick would win in a prime-time fictional fistfight.

SETUP:

  • Place all 52 tiles (2 of each letter A–Z) in the cloth bag.
  • Give each player a pencil and a sheet of paper.
  • Place the sand timer in reach.

GAMEPLAY OVERVIEW:

Each round consists of drawing initials, quickly naming a person, resolving any challenges, writing names under a time limit, revealing and crossing out duplicates, choosing a champion, and debating a winner.

ROUND FLOW (2+ Players):

  1. DRAW INITIALS:
    • Draw 2 tiles from the bag: first is the first name initial, second is the last name initial.
    • If initials are too hard, redraw.
  2. FIRST NAME CALL:
    • First valid name shouted = +1 point.
    • No one else can write it down unless they were the one to say it.
    • If no name within 2 minutes, redraw.
  3. CHALLENGE PHASE (Optional):
    • If someone thinks the first name is invalid, they may call: "CHALLENGE!"
    • Players argue and can't agree.
    • Challenger Googles the person.
    • Challenger asks: "What is their job/occupation, or what is one fact about them?"
    • If challenger is correct:
      • +1 point to challenger.
      • First name is canceled. Round ends immediately.
    • If challenger is wrong:
      • +1 additional point to first-namer.
      • Round ends immediately.
  4. TIMER START (2 MINUTES):
    • All players write as many valid names as they can with the initials.
    • No repeats from previous rounds.
  5. REVEAL & CROSS OUT DUPLICATES:
    • Reveal names. Cross out duplicates.
  6. CHAMPION SELECTION:
    • Each player picks one name to enter the fight.
  7. DEBATE PHASE:
    • 2 minutes to argue, 2 minutes to rebut.
    • All fighters in their prime.
  8. DECIDE WINNER:
    • In 2-player games: If tied, non-first-namer chooses judge or concedes for 1 point.
    • In 3+ player games: group votes.
  9. BONUS POINT:
    • If only one player submitted any names, they get +1 bonus.
  10. SCORE IT:
    • Record round points.

SCORING SUMMARY:

  • +1: First to name a valid person.
  • +1: Winner of the fight (or +2 if judged in 2-player game).
  • +1: Bonus for solo valid entry.
  • +1: Successful challenge of invalid name.

WINNING THE GAME:

First player to reach 15 points wins!

ROUND EXAMPLES:

2-Player Example:

  • Tiles: B + J
  • Player 1: "Billy Joel" (+1)
  • Writes: Billy Joel, Bob Johnson, Boris Johnson
  • Player 2: Betty Jones, Bruce Jenner
  • No duplicates → Player 1: Boris, Player 2: Bruce
  • Bruce wins debate (+1)
Player 1: 1 point
Player 2: 1 point

2-Player Challenge (Unsuccessful):

  • Tiles: M + T
  • Player 1: "Mark Twain" (+1)
  • Player 2: CHALLENGE!
  • Name confirmed valid → Player 1 gets +1 extra
Player 1: 2 points
Player 2: 0 points

2-Player Challenge (Successful):

  • Tiles: D + S
  • Player 1: "Derek Stevens"
  • Player 2: CHALLENGE! → Name invalid
Player 1: 0 points
Player 2: 1 point

2-Player Challenge (Google-Fact):

  • Tiles: B + A
  • Player 1: "Ben Askren" (+1)
  • Player 2: CHALLENGE! → Google shows UFC fighter
  • Challenge fails → Player 1 gets +1 extra
Player 1: 2 points
Player 2: 0 points

2-Player Example (Duplicate Cancelled):

  • Tiles: J + B
  • Player 1: "James Brown" (+1)
  • Player 1 also wrote: Jack Black
  • Player 2 wrote: Jack Black → cancelled
  • Player 2 has no valid names → Player 1 +1 bonus
Player 1: 2 points
Player 2: 0 points

3+ Player Example (Standard):

  • Tiles: S + P
  • Player 2: "Samuel Pepys" (+1)
  • Everyone writes different names → debate happens
Points depend on name, bonus, debate win

3+ Player Example (Duplicates & Invalids):

  • Tiles: M + J
  • Player 1: "Magic Johnson" (+1)
  • All players wrote “Michael Jordan” → cancelled
  • Mary Jane Watson is invalid
  • Player 2 has no names → no fighter
Player 1: 1 point
Player 3: 1 point
Player 2: 0 points

ROUND FLOW CHART:

[DRAW INITIALS] → [FIRST VALID NAME? (CHALLENGE?)] → [CHALLENGE RESOLUTION] → [TIMER] → [WRITE NAMES] → [CROSS OUT DUPLICATES] → [PICK FIGHTER] → [DEBATE] → [WINNER] → [SCORE]

ADDITIONAL RULES:

  • Anyone famous or Google-able is valid.
  • No made-up people
  • No repeats of names during game.
  • All fighters are in their prime.
  • Optional filters: athletes only, villains only, etc.
  • "The" Does not count as an initial as in The Hulk or The Rock
